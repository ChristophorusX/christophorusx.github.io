Proof: Bounded and Unbounded Operators on Lp Space

Posted on 2017-03-21 | In Academic |

Let $\phi:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{C}$ be measurable function and $1\leq p\leq \infty$.

  • If $\phi\in L^\infty(\mathbb{R})$, then the operator $M\phi f=f\phi$ for $f\in L^p(\mathbb{R})$ is a bounded operator from $L^p(\mathbb{R})$ to $L^p(\mathbb{R})$, and its operator norm is precisely $\Vert M\phi\Vert=\Vert\phi\Vert_\infty$.
  • Conversely, if $f\phi\in L^p(\mathbb{R})$ for every $f\in L^p(\mathbb{R})$, then $\phi\in L^\infty(\mathbb{R})$.

Proof: Since $\phi\in L^\infty(\mathbb{R})$ and there is some fixed number $K=\Vert\phi\Vert\infty$, i.e. $\vert\phi\vert\leq K$ a.e., it follows that
$$
\Vert M\phi f\Vert =\Vert f\phi\Vert\leq K\Vert f\Vert=\Vert\phi\Vert\infty\Vert f\Vert
$$
We can get the strict equality because
$$
\Vert M\phi\Vert=\sup{f\in L^\infty(\mathbb{R})}{\Vert M\phi f\Vert\over \Vert f\Vert}
$$
and for any $\varepsilon>0$, there exists a set $E$ with positive measure, s.t. $\vert\phi\vert>K-\varepsilon$. Let $f=\mathbb{I}{E\cap[-l,l]}\in L^p(\mathbb{R})$ and we have
$$
\Vert M\phi f\Vert =\Vert f\phi\Vert\geq\Vert(K-\varepsilon)f\Vert=(K-\varepsilon)\Vert f\Vert
$$
Therefore, we conclude $\Vert M\phi\Vert=\Vert\phi\Vert\infty$.

If $p=\infty$ and for any $f\in L^\infty(\mathbb{R})$ we have $f\phi\in L^\infty(\mathbb{R})$, we claim that $\phi$ must be essentially bounded. Otherwise, $f=\mathbb{I}_\mathbb{R}\in L^\infty(\mathbb{R})$ will give $f\phi=\phi\notin L^\infty(\mathbb{R})$.

If $p\neq\infty$ and $\phi\notin L^\infty(\mathbb{R})$, there are infinitely many $Ek=\lbrace k\leq|\phi|<k+1\rbrace$ with positive measure, each measurable and disjoint. Choose any $E{nk}, k\in \mathbb{N}$, all with positive measure and take $E=\cup E{nk}$. Define
$$
f(x)=
\begin{cases}
{1\over k^{2/p}\mu(E{nk})^{1/p}},\quad x\in E{nk} \
0,\quad x\notin E
\end{cases}
$$
Then
$$
\int|f|^pd\mu=\sum{k=1}^\infty\int_{E{nk}}{1\over k^2\mu(E{nk})}=\sum{k=1}^\infty {1\over k^2}<\infty
$$
and
$$
\int|f\phi|^pd\mu\geq\sum{k=1}^\infty\int{E{nk}}{k^p\over k^2\mu(E{nk})}
$$
As $p\geq1$, it is obvious that RHS gives
$$
\int|f\phi|^pd\mu\geq\sum{k=1}^\infty\int_{E{nk}}{k^p\over k^2\mu(E{nk})}\geq \sum{k=1}^\infty {1\over k}=\infty
$$
Contradiction!
$$
\tag*{$\Box$}
$$
